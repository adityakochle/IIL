## Academic Policy 23-14: Major Change Policy for Transfer Students
**[Page 12]**

### Scope
This policy applies to all undergraduate students who (a) were admitted to SFSU as transfer students and (b) seek to change their declared major after initial enrollment at SFSU.

### Eligibility Requirements for Major Change

**Section 23-14.1:** A transfer student may petition to change their declared major at any time after completing at least one semester in residence at SFSU. However, certain conditions apply:

1. **Impacted Major Threshold:** The student must meet the impaction GPA threshold of the receiving major at the time of the petition. The threshold applied is the **current** threshold, not the threshold at the time of original transfer admission.

2. **Unit Ceiling:** Students who have already accumulated 90 or more units at the time of the major change petition may be required to provide an academic plan demonstrating graduation within two additional semesters. Such plans must be approved by the Dean of the receiving college.

3. **Prerequisite Completion:** The receiving department may require completion of specific prerequisite courses before formally approving the major change. The department has 15 business days to evaluate the petition.

4. **Petition Deadline:** Major change petitions must be submitted no later than the **end of the second week of classes** for the semester in which the student wishes the change to take effect. Late petitions may be processed for the following semester.

### Process
- Students submit a "Change of Major" form via the MyCSUMentor portal
- Form requires departmental approval signature from the chair or designated advisor of the receiving department
- Registrar's Office processes the change within 5 business days of receiving an approved form
- Financial aid implications must be reviewed separately with the Financial Aid Office

### Academic Policy 23-14 — Special Provision for STEM Majors
**[Page 13]**

For impacted STEM majors (including Computer Science, Computer Engineering, and Biology), students petitioning to change into these majors after transfer must additionally:

- Have completed at least one verified prerequisite course in the receiving major at SFSU (not just via articulation), OR
- Provide a signed letter of intent from the department chair affirming that the student has met departmental competency standards

This provision exists because many STEM major prerequisites from community colleges have not been formally articulated, and departments need assurance of foundational competency.

## Academic Policy 21-05: Catalog Rights and Graduation Requirements
**[Page 18]**

### Catalog Year Assignment

Students graduate under the catalog in effect at the time of their **first enrollment at SFSU**, provided they maintain continuous enrollment. This is called the student's "catalog year."

**Continuous Enrollment:** A student maintains continuous enrollment if they are enrolled at SFSU for at least one course in each Fall and Spring semester. Summer enrollment does not break continuous enrollment, nor does an approved Leave of Absence.

**Catalog Year Change:** Students may voluntarily elect to graduate under a more recent catalog by submitting a "Catalog Year Change" form to the Registrar's Office. Once changed, the student may not revert to their original catalog year.

### Graduation Requirements Under Prior Catalogs

If a student's catalog year is prior to the current academic year, they may graduate under older degree requirements. However:

- Any major requirement changes that are safety, accreditation, or law-related are automatically applied regardless of catalog year
- Students must still meet current minimum GPA requirements (2.0 cumulative)
- Financial aid eligibility is calculated based on current enrollment requirements, not catalog-year requirements

## Academic Policy 22-08: Academic Probation and Disqualification
**[Page 25]**

### Conditions for Academic Probation

A student is placed on Academic Probation when their cumulative SFSU GPA falls below 2.0 at the end of any semester. Students on probation receive a formal notification from the Registrar and must meet with their academic advisor within 30 days.

**Probation Impact on Enrollment:**
- Students on Academic Probation are restricted to a maximum enrollment of 13 units per semester
- Priority enrollment is suspended; students enroll on the last day of the enrollment window
- Students may not add courses after the first week of classes while on probation

### Disqualification

A student is subject to Academic Disqualification if:
- They complete a second consecutive semester with cumulative GPA below 2.0, OR
- Their cumulative GPA falls below 1.5 at any point

Disqualified students are administratively withdrawn from all enrolled courses for the following semester. They may petition for reinstatement after one year by demonstrating academic readiness through completion of coursework at another institution.

## Academic Policy 24-01: Withdrawal and Incomplete Grade Policies
**[Page 30]**

### Course Withdrawal Deadlines

| Period | Action Available | Grade Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–2 | Drop with no record | No "W" on transcript |
| Weeks 3–10 | Withdrawal with "W" | "W" appears; not GPA-impacted |
| Weeks 11–15 | Withdrawal requires Dean approval | "W" appears; petition required |
| After Week 15 | No withdrawal; grade assigned | Letter grade calculated |

### Incomplete Grade Policy

An Incomplete ("I") grade may be assigned when:
- A student has completed at least 75% of the course requirements, AND
- An emergency or unforeseen circumstance prevents completion of the remaining work, AND
- The instructor and student agree on a contract for completing remaining work

**Incomplete Grade Deadline:** All incomplete coursework must be submitted within **one year** of the "I" grade assignment. If not resolved, the "I" converts to an "IC" (incomplete/charged), which counts as an F in GPA calculations.

## Academic Policy 19-03: Double Major and Minor Policies
**[Page 38]**

### Double Major
Students may pursue two majors simultaneously if:
- Both majors approve the student's academic plan
- The student can demonstrate a realistic graduation timeline (typically no more than 2 additional semesters beyond the longer major's requirements)
- Combined unit total does not exceed 150 units for financial aid purposes

### Minor Declaration
- Students may declare a minor at any time after their first semester at SFSU
- Minor courses may overlap with major requirements by up to 50% (at most half the minor's units can double-count with major coursework)
- Minors appear on the SFSU transcript and diploma upon graduation

**Applied Math Minor Note:** The Applied Mathematics Minor is particularly compatible with CS, Physics, and Engineering majors due to substantial course overlap. Students should consult the Math Department advisor for an overlap analysis prior to declaring.

## Academic Policy 23-09: Leave of Absence Policy
**[Page 42]**

Students in good academic standing (cumulative GPA ≥ 2.0) may request a Leave of Absence for up to **two consecutive semesters** without losing their enrollment priority or catalog year rights. Conditions:

- Leave must be approved by the student's college Dean before the start of the semester
- Students on Leave of Absence are not eligible for financial aid
- International students on F-1 visas must consult the International Student Services Office; Leave of Absence may affect visa status
- Students returning from Leave must notify the Registrar's Office by the announced deadline to reactivate enrollment
