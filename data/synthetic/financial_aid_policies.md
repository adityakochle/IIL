# Financial Aid Handbook 2024-25
## Office of Student Financial Aid — San Francisco State University

---

## Chapter 1: Eligibility Requirements
**[Page 5]**

### 1.1 Federal Aid Eligibility

To be eligible for federal financial aid (Pell Grant, Direct Loans, Work-Study), students must:

1. Be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen
2. Have a valid Social Security Number
3. Be enrolled in an eligible degree or certificate program
4. Maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
5. Not be in default on any federal student loan
6. Not owe a refund on any federal grant

### 1.2 Enrollment Status Requirements

| Aid Type | Minimum Enrollment |
|---|---|
| Federal Pell Grant | At least 3 units (prorated below 12 units) |
| Cal Grant A/B | Full-time (12+ units) |
| SFSU Institutional Grants | Full-time (12+ units) |
| Federal Direct Loans | At least 6 units (half-time) |
| Federal Work-Study | At least 6 units |
| Subsidized Loan | At least 6 units |

**Note:** Cal Grant recipients who drop below full-time status (12 units) after the census date will have their award prorated. Cal Grant awards are canceled for enrollment below 6 units per semester.

## Chapter 2: Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
**[Page 12]**

### 2.1 SAP Standards

SFSU measures SAP at the end of each payment period (semester) and applies three standards:

**Standard 1: Cumulative GPA**
- Undergraduate students must maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher

**Standard 2: Pace of Completion (Quantitative)**
- Students must successfully complete at least **67%** of all units attempted at SFSU
- Example: A student who has attempted 60 units must have completed (passed) at least 40 units

**Standard 3: Maximum Timeframe**
- Students may not receive financial aid beyond **150% of the published length of the program**
- For a 120-unit degree: maximum = 180 attempted units
- Transfer units count toward attempted units for the purpose of the 150% calculation

### 2.2 Grades That Count Toward SAP

| Grade | Counts as Attempted? | Counts as Completed? |
|---|---|---|
| A, B, C, D | Yes | Yes |
| F | Yes | No |
| W (Withdrawal) | Yes | No |
| I (Incomplete) | Yes | No (until resolved) |
| IC (Incomplete/Charged) | Yes | No |
| NC (No Credit) | Yes | No |
| CR (Credit) | Yes | Yes |
| AU (Audit) | No | No |
| Repeated Course | Yes (all attempts) | Yes (highest passing grade) |

### 2.3 SAP Failure and Appeal Process
**[Page 16]**

Students who fail to meet SAP standards lose eligibility for financial aid for the following semester. Students may appeal SAP disqualification by submitting a SAP Appeal Form to the Financial Aid Office, documenting:

- The extenuating circumstances that caused the failure (illness, family emergency, etc.)
- An academic plan signed by their academic advisor demonstrating how they will meet SAP by the next evaluation

**Appeal Deadline:** SAP appeals must be submitted within 30 days of the notification date. Late appeals are reviewed at the discretion of the Financial Aid Director.

## Chapter 3: Transfer Student Financial Aid
**[Page 20]**

### 3.1 Eligibility Upon Transfer

Transfer students who received financial aid at their prior institution must:
- Submit a new FAFSA or CADAA for the SFSU academic year
- Have their prior institution's aid history reviewed; prior federal loan debt counts toward annual and aggregate loan limits
- Complete entrance counseling if they have not previously received Direct Loans

### 3.2 Transfer Units and SAP Calculation

**Critical Policy:** Transfer units are included in the SAP maximum timeframe calculation (150% rule). A student who transfers with 70 units has already "used" 70 of their allowable attempted units. For a 120-unit degree, the student may only attempt an additional 110 units at SFSU before hitting the 180-unit maximum (70 transfer + 110 SFSU = 180).

**Example for Major-Change Transfer Students:**
- Student transfers with 65 units
- Student changes from Business to CS (longer degree path)
- CS requires 120 units; student has 65 transfer units accepted
- Student must complete ~55 additional units at SFSU
- Maximum attempted units remaining: 180 – 65 = 115 SFSU units allowed
- If student requires 5 semesters × 15 units = 75 units, they remain within limits

### 3.3 Cal Grant Transfer Rules
**[Page 22]**

California residents who receive Cal Grant awards must renew annually through the Cal Grant renewal process (FAFSA/CADAA by March 2 deadline). Transfer students who received Cal Grant at a CCC may continue their award at SFSU subject to:
- Cal Grant eligibility term limits (maximum 4 years of undergraduate-level Cal Grant A)
- GPA requirement: 2.0 minimum for Cal Grant A renewal
- Units attempted: students must be on track to graduate within the remaining grant period

## Chapter 4: Scholarships and Work Programs
**[Page 30]**

### 4.1 SFSU Merit Scholarships for Transfer Students

| Scholarship | Award Amount | GPA Requirement | Application Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Presidential Transfer Scholarship | $5,000/year | 3.7+ transfer GPA | February 1 |
| Dean's Transfer Award | $3,000/year | 3.5–3.69 transfer GPA | February 1 |
| STEM Transfer Excellence Award | $2,500/year | 3.0+ in STEM prerequisites | March 1 |
| First-Generation Transfer Grant | $1,500/year | 2.5+ GPA; first-gen documentation | March 15 |

All scholarships require full-time enrollment (12+ units) to maintain the award. GPA must be maintained at or above the scholarship threshold each semester.

### 4.2 Federal Work-Study (FWS)
**[Page 34]**

Students awarded Federal Work-Study may work up to **20 hours per week** during the academic year. Work-Study positions are available on-campus in university departments and off-campus at approved non-profit or public agencies.

Work-Study earnings are reported on the FAFSA as income for the following year's aid calculation but do not reduce the following year's aid award (a specific Work-Study income exclusion applies).

## Chapter 5: Loan Policies
**[Page 38]**

### 5.1 Federal Direct Loan Annual Limits

| Year | Dependent Students | Independent Students |
|---|---|---|
| 1st Year (0–29 units) | $5,500 | $9,500 |
| 2nd Year (30–59 units) | $6,500 | $10,500 |
| 3rd Year+ (60+ units) | $7,500 | $12,500 |
| Graduate/Professional | N/A | $20,500 |

**Aggregate Loan Limits:**
- Dependent Undergraduates: $31,000 total
- Independent Undergraduates: $57,500 total
- Graduate Students: $138,500 total (including undergraduate borrowing)

### 5.2 Loan Repayment and Exit Counseling

Students who graduate, withdraw, or drop below half-time enrollment must complete **exit counseling** online at studentaid.gov. Loan repayment begins six months after the student leaves school (grace period). Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) plans are available for students whose loan payments exceed 10% of discretionary income.
