## Chapter 4: Requirements for Baccalaureate Degrees

### 4.1 Minimum Unit Requirements
**[Page 55]**

To graduate from SFSU with a baccalaureate degree, students must complete a minimum of **120 semester units**. Some majors require more:

- **Standard degrees:** 120 units minimum
- **Engineering B.S. degrees:** 128 units minimum
- **Architecture B.S.:** 132 units minimum
- **Nursing B.S.N.:** 124 units minimum

No student may graduate with more than **150 units** applied toward a single baccalaureate degree. Units beyond 150 are eligible for financial aid disqualification review.

### 4.2 Minimum GPA Requirements
**[Page 56]**

| GPA Requirement | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Cumulative SFSU GPA | 2.0 |
| Major GPA (courses in major prefix) | 2.0 |
| General Education GPA | 2.0 |
| Overall all-institution GPA (SFSU + transfer) | 2.0 |

A student may not graduate if any of the above GPAs falls below 2.0, regardless of unit completion.

## 4.3 General Education Requirements
**[Page 58]**

SFSU requires all students to fulfill the **CSU General Education Breadth** requirements (39 units) or the **Intersegmental General Education Transfer Curriculum (IGETC)** pattern (37 units for students with an IGETC certification from a CCC).

### IGETC Certification
Transfer students who completed the IGETC pattern at a California Community College and received IGETC certification satisfy the lower-division GE requirements. IGETC does **not** fulfill:
- SFSU's upper-division GE requirement (9 units of upper-division GE at SFSU)
- Major-specific requirements
- United States History, Constitution & American Ideals requirement (if not included in IGETC)

### Upper-Division GE (Required for All Students)
**[Page 60]**

All SFSU students must complete **9 units of upper-division GE** regardless of IGETC status:
- Area UD-B: Physical and/or Life Sciences (3 units, upper-division)
- Area UD-C: Arts and/or Humanities (3 units, upper-division)
- Area UD-D: Social Sciences (3 units, upper-division)

These courses must be taken at SFSU; they do not transfer from other institutions.

## 4.4 Residency Requirement
**[Page 62]**

As described in the Transfer Credit Policy (Chapter 7), students must complete:

- **30 units in residence** at SFSU
- At least **12 of the 30 residency units in the student's major**
- A minimum of **24 units of upper-division coursework** at SFSU (included within the 30-unit residency requirement)

**Exception:** Students participating in SFSU-approved study abroad or National Student Exchange programs may count up to 9 units of the 30-unit residency requirement from those programs.

## 4.5 Application for Graduation
**[Page 63]**

Students must formally apply for graduation through the Registrar's Office. Application deadlines:

| Graduation Term | Application Deadline |
|---|---|
| May (Spring) | October 1 of prior Fall |
| August (Summer) | February 1 |
| December (Fall) | March 1 |

**Late applications** incur a $25 processing fee and are accepted until the end of Week 6 of the graduation semester. Applications submitted after Week 6 are processed for the next term.

### Graduation with Honors
**[Page 64]**

| Distinction | Cumulative GPA Requirement |
|---|---|
| Cum Laude | 3.5–3.699 |
| Magna Cum Laude | 3.7–3.899 |
| Summa Cum Laude | 3.9 and above |

To be eligible for honors distinction, students must have completed a minimum of **60 units at SFSU** (not just in residence—all SFSU-enrolled units). Transfer units do not count toward the 60-unit minimum for honors eligibility.

## 4.6 Second Baccalaureate Degree
**[Page 66]**

A student who already holds a baccalaureate degree (from any accredited institution) may earn a second baccalaureate degree from SFSU under the following conditions:

- Complete a minimum of **30 units in residence at SFSU** for the second degree
- Satisfy all requirements for the second major
- Maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or above
- Prior baccalaureate units may be applied toward GE requirements for the second degree

## 4.7 Simultaneous Degrees and Double Majors
**[Page 67]**

### Double Major (Single Degree)
Students completing two majors within the same degree type (e.g., two B.S. majors, or two B.A. majors) receive a single diploma noting both majors. Both majors must be completed with minimum GPA requirements. No additional residency units are required beyond the standard 30.

### Simultaneous Degrees (Two Degrees)
Students pursuing two different degree types (e.g., B.S. and B.A.) must:
- Complete at least **150 total units**
- Satisfy all requirements for both degree programs independently
- Complete at least **30 units** beyond the requirements of the first degree for the second degree

## 4.8 Enrollment Status and Time-to-Degree
**[Page 70]**

### Full-Time vs. Part-Time Status
- **Full-Time:** 12 or more units per semester
- **Part-Time:** fewer than 12 units per semester
- Financial aid typically requires full-time enrollment (12+ units); verify with Financial Aid Office for program-specific requirements

### Maximum Time-to-Degree Standards
SFSU follows CSU system policy on degree completion timelines. Students who have not completed their degree within **10 years** of initial enrollment are subject to degree plan review and may be required to update their coursework to current catalog requirements regardless of catalog year rights.

### Overload Policy
Students with a cumulative SFSU GPA of 3.0 or higher may request enrollment in more than 18 units per semester. Requests require Dean's approval and are granted for a maximum of 21 units per semester.

---

## 4.9 Exit Requirements and Commencement
**[Page 75]**

### Commencement Ceremony
SFSU holds commencement ceremonies twice annually: May (for Spring graduates) and December (for Fall graduates). Participation in commencement is open to students who:
- Have completed all degree requirements, OR
- Have 6 or fewer units remaining in their final semester and have filed a graduation application

### Diploma Processing
Diplomas are mailed 8–10 weeks after the close of the graduation term. Students may request an official letter of degree completion from the Registrar's Office immediately after graduation is certified.
