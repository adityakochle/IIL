# Computer Science — Bachelor of Science
## Department of Computer Science, College of Science & Engineering
### San Francisco State University — Academic Catalog 2024-25
**Pages 210–228**

---

## Overview

The Computer Science B.S. program at SFSU prepares students for careers in software engineering, systems design, artificial intelligence, and related fields. The degree requires **120 total units**, including major requirements, general education, and elective coursework.

**Degree Type:** Bachelor of Science
**College:** Science & Engineering
**Department:** Computer Science
**Total Units Required:** 120
**Minimum Major GPA:** 2.0 (C or better in all major courses)

---

## Prerequisites for Major Admission
**[Page 211]**

Transfer students seeking CS major admission must present the following completed coursework (or equivalent SFSU courses) prior to or at the time of transfer:

| Course | SFSU Equivalent | Units | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Calculus 1 | Math 124 | 4 | Required before CS 211 |
| Calculus 2 | Math 125 | 4 | Required before CS 340 |
| Introduction to Programming | CS 101 | 3 | Gateway course |
| Discrete Mathematics | CS 230 | 3 | Required before CS 311 |

**Policy Note:** Students who have not completed CS 101 equivalent prior to transfer must enroll in CS 101 during their first semester at SFSU before progressing to upper-division CS coursework.

## Lower-Division Major Requirements
**[Page 212]**

### Core Sequence (Required)

| Course | Title | Units | Prerequisite(s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| CS 101 | Introduction to Computing | 3 | None |
| CS 110 | Introduction to Programming | 3 | CS 101 or placement |
| CS 211 | Object-Oriented Programming | 3 | CS 110, Math 124 |
| CS 230 | Discrete Mathematics for CS | 3 | Math 124 (may be concurrent) |
| CS 240 | Introduction to Data Structures | 3 | CS 211 |

**Critical Prerequisite Chain:** CS 101 → CS 110 → CS 211 → CS 240 → CS 311 → CS 412

Transfer students who bypass CS 101 via articulation can start at CS 110, allowing faster progression. However, students must verify CS 101 articulation with the CS Department before assuming credit is granted.

## Upper-Division Major Requirements
**[Page 215]**

### Required Core (All Courses Mandatory)

| Course | Title | Units | Prerequisite(s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| CS 311 | Algorithm Design and Analysis | 3 | CS 240, CS 230 |
| CS 340 | Programming Languages | 3 | CS 211, Math 125 |

**Senior Capstone Note:** CS 499 is offered in Fall and Spring. Students must apply for the capstone in the semester prior to enrollment. The course is team-based and graded on a pass/no-credit basis.

### Upper-Division Elective Requirements
**[Page 216]**

Students must complete **18 units** of upper-division CS electives, selecting from the following concentrations:

**Concentration A: Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning**
- CS 431: Machine Learning (3 units; prereq: Math 245 Linear Algebra)
- CS 432: Deep Learning (3 units; prereq: CS 431)
- CS 469: Natural Language Processing (3 units; prereq: CS 431)

**Concentration B: Systems & Networks**
- CS 450: Computer Networks (3 units; prereq: CS 412)
- CS 455: Distributed Systems (3 units; prereq: CS 412)
- CS 461: Database Systems (3 units; prereq: CS 311)

**Concentration C: Human-Computer Interaction**
- CS 410: Human-Computer Interaction (3 units; prereq: CS 311)
- CS 477: Mobile Application Development (3 units; prereq: CS 476)

**Important:** Math 226 (Calculus III) is required for the B.S. in Computer Science. Students who transfer with Calculus I and II (Math 124/125 equivalents) still need to complete Math 226 at SFSU. This course is typically offered every semester but has enrollment caps of 30 students.

## Estimated Timeline for Transfer Students
**[Page 220]**

### Scenario A: Transfer with 60 Units, CS Prerequisites Complete
- **Semester 1:** CS 311, CS 340, Math 226, GE elective (13 units)
- **Semester 2:** CS 351, CS 412, Upper-div elective x2 (12 units)
- **Semester 3:** CS 476, CS 499, Upper-div elective x2 (12 units)
- **Semester 4:** Remaining electives + GE (12 units)
- **Total:** 4 semesters (2 years)

### Scenario B: Transfer with 65 Units, Missing Math 226
- **Semester 1:** CS 311, Math 226, CS 340 (10 units — Math 226 must be concurrent with CS 340 prereqs)
- **Semester 2:** CS 351, CS 412, upper-div elective (9 units)
- **Semester 3:** CS 476, upper-div electives (9 units)
- **Semester 4:** CS 499, remaining electives (9 units)
- **Semester 5:** Final GE and remaining units (if applicable)
- **Estimated Total:** 4.5–5 semesters (approximately 2.5 years)

**Note for Major Changers:** Students switching to CS after initial enrollment in another major must formally declare the change through the Office of the Registrar and meet the CS Department's impaction threshold (3.0 GPA in college-level math and CS courses attempted at SFSU).

## GPA Requirements and Academic Standing
**[Page 222]**

- Minimum cumulative GPA: 2.0 for graduation
- Minimum major GPA: 2.0 (no individual major course grade below C)
- Students with major GPA below 2.0 are placed on Departmental Academic Probation and must meet with the CS advisor within 30 days
- Students on Departmental Academic Probation for two consecutive semesters may be disqualified from the major

## Applied Mathematics Minor — Complementary Option
**[Page 224]**

The Applied Mathematics Minor (18 units) is available to students in any major. For CS transfer students with strong math backgrounds, this minor can be completed alongside the CS major with minimal additional coursework beyond what is already required.

**Applied Math Minor Requirements:**
- Math 124: Calculus I (4 units) — likely already completed
- Math 125: Calculus II (4 units) — likely already completed
- Math 226: Calculus III (4 units) — required for CS B.S., double-counts
- Math 324: Probability and Statistics (3 units) — required for CS
- Math 370: Introduction to Real Analysis (3 units) — additional elective

**For transfer students with Calc I and II already completed:** Declaring the Applied Math Minor requires only Math 226 (already required for CS B.S.) and Math 370 as additional coursework — adding approximately 3 units beyond the CS degree requirements.
