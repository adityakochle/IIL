**Pages 140–148**

## 7.1 Maximum Transferable Units

### 7.1.1 From California Community Colleges (CCC)
**[Page 142]**

Per California State University (CSU) system-wide policy (Executive Order 1100), SFSU accepts a maximum of **70 semester units** from California Community Colleges toward the undergraduate degree. Units earned beyond 70 will not apply toward SFSU degree requirements, although they may satisfy prerequisite requirements.

Students presenting more than 70 units from a CCC will be evaluated as follows:
- Only the first 70 units (by date of completion) count toward the degree unit total
- Excess units do not count toward upper-division residency requirements
- Coursework above 70 units may still be evaluated for prerequisite clearance

**Example:** A student transferring 78 CCC units will have 70 units applied to their SFSU degree. The remaining 8 units are acknowledged but do not count toward degree completion.

### 7.1.2 From Four-Year Institutions
**[Page 143]**

Transfer students from accredited four-year colleges or universities may transfer a maximum of **70 units** of lower-division coursework. Upper-division units transfer without a cap, subject to course equivalency evaluation by the Office of the Registrar.

Students with coursework from both a CCC and a four-year institution are subject to a combined maximum of **90 total transfer units** applicable toward an SFSU degree.

## 7.2 Transfer Admission Requirements

### 7.2.1 Impacted Major Thresholds
**[Page 144]**

SFSU maintains impaction thresholds for high-demand majors. Transfer students must meet major-specific GPA requirements in addition to the CSU minimum 2.0 GPA:

| Major | Minimum Transfer GPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Computer Science (B.S.) | 3.0 | Competitive; capacity-limited |
| Computer Engineering | 2.8 | |
| Nursing | 3.2 | NCLEX prerequisite tracking required |
| Business Administration | 2.7 | |
| Psychology | 2.5 | General prerequisite screening |
| Liberal Studies | 2.0 | No impaction threshold |

**Academic Policy 23-14:** Students changing their major after admission as a transfer student must meet the receiving major's impaction threshold at the time of the major change, not the threshold at the time of initial transfer admission.

### 7.2.2 Unit Completion Requirement for Transfer Admission
**[Page 144]**

To be eligible for transfer admission, students must complete:
- A minimum of **60 transferable semester units** (or 90 quarter units) by the end of the spring term prior to fall enrollment
- The CSU-required 4-course, 16-unit general education breadth pattern ("Golden Four"): English composition, critical thinking/composition, mathematics/quantitative reasoning, and oral communication

## 7.3 Course Equivalency and Articulation

### 7.3.1 ASSIST Articulation Agreements
**[Page 145]**

Course equivalency between SFSU and California Community Colleges is governed by formal articulation agreements accessible via **ASSIST.org** (Articulation System Stimulating Interinstitutional Student Transfer). Articulation agreements are reviewed annually; students are advised to consult the ASSIST agreement for the **academic year in which their courses were completed**, not the current year.

Key principles:
- Articulated courses satisfy SFSU major or GE requirements as specified
- Non-articulated courses undergo manual review by the relevant department chair
- Retroactive articulation is not granted; students should verify equivalency before enrollment

### 7.3.2 AP, IB, and CLEP Credit
**[Page 146]**

Advanced Placement (AP), International Baccalaureate (IB), and College-Level Examination Program (CLEP) scores are evaluated as follows:

| Exam | Minimum Score | SFSU Credit |
|---|---|---|
| AP Calculus AB | 3 | Math 124 (4 units) |
| AP Calculus BC | 3 | Math 124 + Math 125 (8 units) |
| AP Computer Science A | 3 | CS 101 (3 units) |
| AP Statistics | 3 | Math 124 equivalency for some majors |
| IB Mathematics HL | 5 | Math 124 (4 units) |
| IB Computer Science HL | 5 | CS 101 (3 units) |

**Note:** AP/IB credits count toward the 70-unit transfer maximum.

## 7.4 Repeat Course Policy for Transfer Students
**[Page 147]**

If a student transfers with a course they have also completed at SFSU (or vice versa), the following rules apply:

- Only one instance of the course (the higher grade) will be counted for GPA computation at SFSU
- Both grades appear on the official transcript
- The student must petition the Registrar's Office if they believe an error exists in repeat-course processing
- Courses repeated more than twice require Dean's Office approval before additional enrollment is permitted

---

## 7.5 Residency Requirement for Transfer Students
**[Page 148]**

All SFSU graduates must complete a minimum of **30 units in residence at SFSU** (upper-division coursework taken while enrolled at SFSU). Transfer students are subject to the same residency requirement.

- At least 12 of the 30 residency units must be completed in the student's major
- Internship, cooperative education, and study-abroad units taken through SFSU-approved programs count toward residency
- Units completed at other CSU campuses via cross-enrollment do **not** count toward SFSU residency
